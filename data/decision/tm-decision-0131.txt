One aspect of the criminal justice system that has not received much attention after George Zimmerman was acquitted in the death of Trayvon Martin is the role that race may play in whether a death-by-firearm is ruled justifiable. Black Americans are far less likely to be adjudicated as justified in using deadly force in a firearm-related death.
