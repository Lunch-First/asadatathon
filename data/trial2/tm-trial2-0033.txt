A JURY'S decision to acquit a 29-year-old Florida man of the murder of an unarmed black teenager has reignited race relations in the US with Miami police bracing for possible riots. 
The jury of six all women, and five of them white took just 16 hours to find Neighbourhood Watch volunteer George Zimmerman not guilty of the charge of second degree murder of 17-year-old Trayvon Martin in February 2012. The verdict sparked angry scenes outside the courthouse and a wave of death threats at Zimmerman, who is of Hispanic descent.
Benjamin Crump, the attorney for the Martin family, said: ``For Trayvon to rest in peace, we must all be peaceful.''
