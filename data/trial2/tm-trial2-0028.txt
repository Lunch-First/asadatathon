The nation exploded Sunday in reaction to the acquittal of George Zimmerman in the shooting death of black teenager Trayvon Martin.
In Union Square, thousands of New Yorkers gathered in protest, and called for civil rights charges to be brought against Zimmerman.
President Obama told Americans to "ask ourselves if we're doing all we can to stem the tide of gun violence that claims too many lives across this country on a daily basis."
Behind it all, though, is the shameful history of racial violence in America.
